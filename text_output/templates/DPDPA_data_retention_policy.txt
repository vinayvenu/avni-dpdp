1.1 INTRODUCTION
This Data Retention Policy ("Policy") is established by [Insert Organisation Name] to ensure
the lawful, fair, and transparent handling of personal data in compliance with the Digital
Personal Data Protection Act, 2023 ("DPDPA"), the Information Technology Act, 2000, and
other applicable laws and regulations. This Policy sets out the principles and procedures
governing the retention and secure disposal of data processed by the Organisation.

1.2 OBJECTIVES
1. Ensure compliance with legal, regulatory, and contractual obligations related to data
retention.
2. Protect the privacy and security of personal data throughout its lifecycle.
3. Provide clear guidelines for the retention, archiving, and destruction of data.
4. Minimise risks associated with over-retention, such as legal liabilities and security
breaches.

1.3 SCOPE
This Policy applies to:

Data Types: Personal data, sensitive personal data, business records, operational data,
and other information processed by the Organisation, regardless of format (electronic or
physical).
Entities Covered: All employees, contractors, vendors, and third parties processing data
on behalf of the Organisation.
Systems: Applications, databases, storage media, and systems used by the Organisation.

1.4 DATA RETENTION PRINCIPLES

1. Purpose Limitation: Retain data only for as long as necessary to fulfill its original
purpose, as outlined in privacy notices or agreements.
2. Retention Periods: Retention periods are based on legal, regulatory, contractual, and
operational requirements, as detailed in the Data Retention Schedule (Annexure A).
3. Data Minimisation: Retain only data necessary for specific purposes.
4. Access Controls: Limit access to retained data to authorised personnel.
5. Secure Disposal: Ensure secure destruction or anonymisation of data no longer required.

1.4 RESPONSIBILITIES
1. Data Protection Officer (DPO):
o Oversee Policy compliance and periodic reviews of the Data Retention Schedule.
o Address queries related to data retention and disposal.
2. Department Heads:
o Ensure departmental compliance with the Policy.
o Identify and classify data within their areas of responsibility.
3. Employees and Contractors:
o Adhere to data retention guidelines.
o Report non-compliance or data incidents to the DPO.

1.8 AUDIT AND MONITORING
1. Internal Audits: Conduct regular audits to ensure adherence to the Policy and Data
Retention Schedule.
2. Non-Compliance: Report instances of non-compliance to the DPO and address them
promptly, with potential disciplinary actions.

changes.

1.9 EXCEPTIONS
Any exceptions to retention or disposal requirements must be:
1. Approved by the DPO.
2. Documented with a rationale for the exception.
